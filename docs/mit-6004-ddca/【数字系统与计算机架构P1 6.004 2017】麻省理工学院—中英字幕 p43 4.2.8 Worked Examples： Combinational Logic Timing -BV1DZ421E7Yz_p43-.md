The propagation delay of a circuit is defined as the longest delay that can occur from when the inputs changed to when the output becomes stable。



![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_9.png)

![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_10.png)

In order to calculate the propagation delay of a circuit。

 we need to identify the path from input to output。

 whose sum of the propagation delays is the largest。



![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_12.png)

![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_13.png)

For this circuit， the longest delayed path is through the exclusive orgate and the multipleer。



![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_15.png)

The sum of the propagation delay across these two gates is 2。1 plus 1。5， which equals 3。

6 nanoseconds。

![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_17.png)

The contamination delay of a circuit is defined as the shortest delay from when the inputs change to when the outputs begin to change or when the outputs are no longer guaranteed to be holding their previous stable value。



![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_19.png)

![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_20.png)

![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_21.png)

In order to calculate the contamination delay， we find the path whose some of the contamination delays is the least。



![](img/c67339ea9bee126865aacb038e934ee4_23.png)

For this circuit， the shortest path is from input A through the selector of the multipleer。

 so the contamination delay for the circuit is equal to the contamination delay of the multipleer。

 which equals 0。2 nanoseconds。
