Bullion logic can be used to simplify logic equations into their simplest form。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_2.png)

This simplest form is known as the function's minimal sum of products representation。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_4.png)

Having a simplified equation allows us to implement the function using the fewest number of gates。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_6.png)

One commonly used method for taking any Boolean expression and converting it to its minimal sum of products equivalent is the use of carno maps。

 The way carno maps work is as follows。 You start with the original truth table specification of a function。

 You then express each set of inputs that makes the function equal to one as a basic product term。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_8.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_9.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_10.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_11.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_12.png)

Oing all of these product terms together produces a sum of product representation for the function。

 Note that this representation is not yet minimal。

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_14.png)

For our example， the sum of products is F equals not A not B， not C， or not AB C， or A not B， not C。

 or A not B C， or ABC。

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_16.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_17.png)

We then create the carno map for the function by making a2 D grid。

 representing all possible input combinations and ensuring that from one column or row to the next in the grid。

 only one single input can be changed。 This is known as gray code。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_19.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_20.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_21.png)

Once we label a car no map， we can fill it with ones for each combination that produces a one output and zero for each combination that produces a zero output。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_23.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_24.png)

Next， we try to merge as many adjacent ones as possible into groups that are of size that is a power of two。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_26.png)

Note that adjacent ones can be merged across rows along columns and across the edges of the carno map like a tous。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_28.png)

Every largest grouping of ones that is required in order to cover all of the ones becomes one term in the minimal sum of product representation of the function。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_30.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_31.png)

To figure out what that term is， look at the labels of all the columns and rows that are covered by the grouping of ones and eliminate all inputs that appear as both a1 and 0 input。

 because these inputs are represented in both their0 and one form。

 It means that that input is not affecting the result of that term。 and can thus be eliminated。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_33.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_34.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_35.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_36.png)

By doing this for all groupings of ones and then oring the results together。

 one produces a minimal sum of product representation for the function。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_38.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_39.png)

Note that having overlapping ones in your groupings is allowed and desired if it will result in a simpler product term。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_41.png)

In our example， we circle the bottom two middle ones。

 which represent the term B C because a appears in both its low and high form in the grouping。

 The next set of ones are the two in the rightmost column。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_43.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_44.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_45.png)

These ones represent the term A not B。 Finally， our last grouping wraps around the first row to create the term not B。

 not C。

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_47.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_48.png)

This is a minimal sum of product representation of our function。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_50.png)

Note， however， that instead of grouping the two ones in the rightmost column。

 we could have instead grouped the two rightmost ones in the bottom row。

 That would have produced the term A C， instead of A not B。



![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_52.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_53.png)

![](img/4b38d686fbc50bef3ab28612964f2ee6_54.png)

Either combination of terms is a valid minimal sum of product representation for this function。
