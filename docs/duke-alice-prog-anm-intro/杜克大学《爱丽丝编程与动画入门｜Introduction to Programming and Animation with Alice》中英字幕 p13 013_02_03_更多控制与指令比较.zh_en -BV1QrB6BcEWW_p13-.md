In acclimating ourselves to animation in Alice， we're going to be looking at several combinations of instructions or procedures that can be run in Alice and how they work。

The first group of procedures are the translational motion procedures。

Translational motion is a rather big way of saying ordinary movement。

Objects can move along three axes in six different directions。The forward backward axis。

 the right left axis， and the up down axis。Rotational motion procedures are all of the ways an object can be turned or rolled。

Like translational motion， rotational motion can be accomplished in three ways。

 in six different directions。The first way is turning forward or backward。

The best analogy to turning forwards is what happens when I try to snow ski。

My skis are long and get caught under the snow， causing me to fall face first and face forward on my skis and into the snow。

The second way is turning right or left。This type of rotation can best be illustrated by opening and closing a door。

The third type of rotation is rolling right or left。

This is analogous to the rolling of a door handle。It is possible to invoke animations on object parts。

 as well as on objects。However， you should only use turn or roll on object parts。

 attemptingtemping to move an object's part results in a very strange distortion as the part is attached to the object。

Next， we'll take a look at some of the differences between doing motion and rotation instructions in order versus doing motion and rotation instructions together。

Finally， we need to look at several instructions that cause an object to move。

The first is the regular move instruction。 It is often preceded by a turn to face or a point at instruction。

Turn to face causes one object to turn to face another object。

Then when the first object next moves forward， it will be moving towards the second object while remaining on the ground。

Turn to face， followed by move is most commonly used when people move as they need to stay on the ground。

Point at， followed by a move， causes the first object to point at the second object。

When the first object moves forward， it moves towards a second object。

 but potentially not staying on the ground。This sequence of instructions works well for birds or fish and not so well for people。

In addition to the regular move instruction， there are three instructions that move an object towards another object。

These are place， move towards and move two， depending on which specific motion is desired。

 they may be better or worse than the regular move instruction。
