In our last session， we covered screening and interviewing candidates。 Now。

 it's time to narrow the candidate pool even further through evaluating and finally selecting prospective employees before we can make a selection。

 There are a few more evaluation steps to help find our best candidate。

 Selecting a candidate is the final level of our selection process pyramid。

 You want to know as much as possible about a candidate's ability to perform the specified job tests。

 and you also want to make sure that the employee poses no obvious risk to workplace place safety or your organization's finances。

😊。

![](img/b4a779ee74cb6edf03d4577ec8ded05e_2.png)

There are a few litmus tests and checks that the candidates should pass before they are hired。

 The first is making sure the testing or checks are related to the position you are selecting for。

 The next requirement is that any tests are equally administered to all possible candidates。

 For example， you might ask a potential call center based customer service representative to take a typing test。

 but they would not need to take a mathematics competency test。

 A mathematics competency test would be appropriate for a cashier who。

 in turn would not necessarily need to take the typing test。

 Neither of these tests should be unnecessarily difficult。

 They are used as a way for the candidate to demonstrate the skills they already have before official hiring and training begins。



![](img/b4a779ee74cb6edf03d4577ec8ded05e_4.png)
