 As you've previously learned， compensating workers for their effort is an integral part of HR work。

 When you're working in an HR role， paying employees is probably going to be something you're task of doing。

 Let's discuss the three different types of payment， salary， wages and differential pay。

Having a basic understanding of these terms and how they are different from each other will make it easier to understand concepts in the rest of this course。

 First， let's define salary。

![](img/a9933adc013ebeeedbcf33e98d7fe8f8_2.png)

A salary is a set compensation paid regularly， typicallyypically。

 a salary is expressed as the amount of money someone makes over a year。

A salary is most common for professionals and white collar workers and is usually paid monthly or biweekly。

Wages are another way to discuss compensation。Wages are a payment for work。

 usually on an hourly or daily basis。 Typically， wages are described as a monetary amount earned per hour or day。

Wages are common for manual or unskilled labor。Finally， let's define differential pay。

Differential pay is additional money paid above the base rate。

 There are several circumstances in which employees may find it necessary to offer differential pay。

 For instance， to motivate employees to accept a specific work assignment， shift or job situation。

 except for over time， differential pay is not required by federal law。
