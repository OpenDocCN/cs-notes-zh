Compensation is defined as everything an employer receives for working。

 including pay and non monetary benefits。An organization has three primary concerns when deciding worker compensation。

 Let's explore each of these。 First， organizations must consider whether their pay is competitive。

 This means they pay their workers a fair market wage relative to other organizations in the same industry and location。

 Failure to offer competitive pay can result in high turnover rates。

 difficulty attracting top talent and low worker morale。



![](img/b3548b00afa59e8a05f2431dee6606c3_4.png)

Another essential consideration is pay equity， which ensures workers with similar roles and responsibilities receive equal compensation。

Organizations can achieve pay equity by establishing clear job classifications and pay grades and regularly reviewing and adjusting worker salaries to maintain equity。

 The final consideration when setting worker pay is legal compliance。

 Organs must ensure that their pay practices comply with all applicable laws and regulations。

 including those related to minimum wage， overtime pay and equal pay for equal work。
