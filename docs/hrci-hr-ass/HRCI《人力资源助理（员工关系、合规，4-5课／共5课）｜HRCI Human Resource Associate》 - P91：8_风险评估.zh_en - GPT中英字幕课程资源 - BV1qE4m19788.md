Risk assessment and mitigation techniques help to promote a safe， secure and compliant workplace。

In this video， you'll learn about assessing risks for your organization。



![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_2.png)

There are many types of risks that an organization might encounter， for example。

 one organization might need emergency evacuation procedures for a wildfire。

 while another might need a business continuity plan for major snowstorms that knock out their communication system for several days。



![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_4.png)

Organizations develop these plans based on an in-depth risk assessment。

 a risk assessment which can also be known as a needs analysis or a threat assessment。

 is a type of analysis that allows organizations to consider the types of risk they might encounter and the possibility of them occurring。

Organizations can use analysis to develop plans to mitigate risk a risk assessment can use different methods。

 including self inspections， injury and accident data， historical data and incidence rates。

A safety self inspection document helps organizations identify hazards and take corrective steps。

 The goal of this assessment is to correct hazards and prevent incidents from happening。A hazard。

 according to OSHA， is defined as the potential for harm。

 The findings from a self inspection can be used to accomplish several goals。 First。

 it can communicate hazards to affected employees。 Next。

 it can be used to coach new employees on safe workplace practices and procedures。



![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_6.png)

It can also be used to discipline employees for unsafe practices or failing to follow safety procedures or to document unsafe conditions and abatement efforts。

To add， it can identify the need for a new or modified safety procedure or rules。Similarly。

 it can assist employers in recommending personal protective equipment。Finally。

 self inspections can prepare an organization for a formal inspection from a government agency。

 customer， or insurance provider。

![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_8.png)

An employer can also conduct a job hazard analysis as a part of risk assessment。

This type of analysis evaluates hazards that are job specific。

 a hazard analysis allows employers to take the necessary steps to control exposure to the risk。

 which can reduce the likelihood of an injury， illness or accident。



![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_10.png)

When conducting a job hazard analysis， it's important to involve the employee and their supervisor who conduct the tasks regularly。

These employees will have the best understanding of the potential risk associated with the environment or equipment and can contribute suggestions to control or eliminate the hazard。



![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_12.png)

A third tool in risk assessment is to collect and analyze injury and accident data。

This data documents all incidents， including injuries， accidents， and near misses。

Reviewing the data is a good way for HR to identify the threats and exposure to all workers in the organization。

Inciiddent investigations are typically conducted by a supervisor or member of a safety committee。

 they focus on determining if an unsafe act or condition has caused injury， accident， or a near miss。

The information collected is then used to implement preventative efforts。

These efforts can focus on eliminating an unsafe condition or addressing an unsafe act through training。

 coaching and discipline if needed。 Another method to assess risk is to review historical data Through past incidents。

 patterns tend to emerge。 OSHA collects data pertaining to the most commonly violated standards by employers and is a good resource for this method。

 The final strategy to risk assessment is to analyze incidence rates。 According to OSHA。

 an incidence rate is the number or frequency of injurious illnesses or loss work dayss per 100 full time workers。



![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_14.png)

![](img/a9b7b05540b0828c7217ad6b98aba8fd_15.png)

You can use this formula to calculate incidence rates， n at times 200，000 divided by EH。

N equals the number of injuries and illnesses， or the number of workdays。 E。

 H is the total hours worked by all employees during a month， quarter or fiscal year。

200000 is a base for 100 full time equivalent workers during 40 hours per week and 50 weeks per year。
