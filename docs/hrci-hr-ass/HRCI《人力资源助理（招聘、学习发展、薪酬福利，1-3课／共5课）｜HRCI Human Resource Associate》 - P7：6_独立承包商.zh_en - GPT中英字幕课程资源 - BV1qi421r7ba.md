First， let's discuss independent contractors。Independent contractors are individuals who agree or contract to do specific work for other people or organizations。

 they are not considered employees independentdependent contractors may work on a project to project basis and are possibly employed by more than one organization or client。

😊，In the US， both the Internal Revenue Service or IRS and the Department of Labor have metrics you can use to determine if someone is an employee or an independent contractor。

 The IRS uses a 20 factor test from form S S 8。 The Department of Labor consider 7 factors。

 Both metrics differ in phrasing and depth of analysis。 but they describe similar situations。

 Later in this course， I'll provide resources that explain these two tests in greater detail。



![](img/45e4800321f911365a8359d0389d09f0_5.png)

These distinctions between employees and independent contractors are important because employers have different obligations to each。

 Let's compare two workers at the same organization。

 Erin is an employee and Irving is an independent contractor Employers must pay half of an employee's Social Security or FIC tax。

Independent contractors must pay all of their own Social Security taxes。

Employers must withhold federal and state taxes from employees。

 Employers are not legally required to withhold federal and state taxes for independent contractors。

Employers must pay employees over time and on call pay employers are not required to pay independent contractors overtime and on call pay depending on the situation and the needs of your organization。

 employing independent contractors might be very beneficial。
