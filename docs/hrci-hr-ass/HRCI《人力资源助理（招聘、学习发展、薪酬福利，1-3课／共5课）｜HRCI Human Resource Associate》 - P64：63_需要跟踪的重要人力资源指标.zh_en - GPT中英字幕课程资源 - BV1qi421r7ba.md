 Let's start with some common metrics that support the hiring process。

 The first metric we'll discuss is cost per hire。 Cos per hire is the amount of money needed to recruit a new employee。

 including fees for advertising， recruiting， Refs， travel and relocation。 This metric is an average。

 You calculate the cost per hire by adding all expenses。

 and dividing the sum by the total number of new hires。😊。



![](img/dea727073f9c25622af29c10165ad915_2.png)

For example， your organization hired three new employees last year。

 The total hiring costs for the first employee was $1500。 This includes $200 for a job advertisement。

$300 to fly the candidate in for an interview and 1000 for relocation。

 The cost to help the candidate move。 The total cost for the second employee was $1600。

 and the cost for the third was $1200。 to find the cost per hire you add the hiring cost for all three employees。

 So $1500 plus 1600 plus $1200 for a total of $4300。

 Then divide that sum of $4300 by the $3 new hires。 The cost per hire for your organization is $1433。

😊。

![](img/dea727073f9c25622af29c10165ad915_4.png)

Your organization can use the cost per higher metric to examine the efficiency of the hiring process。

 The results may determine if expenses during the hiring or onboarding process are accurate。

 or should be adjusted。😊。

The next metric is time to fill。 Time to fill measures the average number of days that a job posting remains open。

 In other words， how long it takes to hire a new employee for a position。

 The starting point may differ by organization。 Some organizations might use the submission of the job posting as the starting point。

 while other organizations might use HR's approval of the job opening as the first day。😊。



![](img/dea727073f9c25622af29c10165ad915_8.png)

To calculate this metric， add together the number of days。 All job positions remain open。 Next。

 divide the sum by the number of positions that you filled。 For example。

 your team filled three positions in the last year。 The first position was open for 15 days。

 The second for 22 days。 and the third for 26 days。 Add up all these days for a total of 63 days。

 Then divide by the three positions that were filled。 your time to fill is 21 days。😊。



![](img/dea727073f9c25622af29c10165ad915_10.png)

If time to fill is too long， improvements and efficiencies in the hiring process are most likely needed。



![](img/dea727073f9c25622af29c10165ad915_12.png)

The final metric is the selection ratio， the selection ratio is the number of applicants hired for a position relative to the number of total applications。



![](img/dea727073f9c25622af29c10165ad915_14.png)

A selection ratio can be useful for evaluating recruiting strategies。

 If the number of hires is high compared to the number of applicants。

 this may indicate that not enough applicants were recruited。😊。

A low number of applicants makes it hard to determine if the best most suited applicants were offered positions。

For example， the last 10 job postings that you filled had a total of 30 applicants。

 The selection ratio for these positions is 10 to 30， or  one to 3。

 This ratio means you had an average of three applicants per position。

 This is a low number of applicants。 you may not have found the best candidate for the position。

 It would be best to reevaluate how you recruit for job positions in the future。😊。
