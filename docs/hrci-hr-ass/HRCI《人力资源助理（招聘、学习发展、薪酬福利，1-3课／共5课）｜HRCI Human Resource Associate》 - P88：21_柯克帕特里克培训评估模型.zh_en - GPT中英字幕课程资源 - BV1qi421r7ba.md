In this video you will learn about the Kirkpatrick model。

 another model used to design employee training The Kirkpatrick model is globally recognized for the evaluation of training outcomes The Kirkpatrick model is defined as having four levels of evaluation for training programs。

 reaction， learning， behavior and results。

![](img/1a2140ef8238c5a639d8d253c0077b45_2.png)

The first level is reaction at the reaction level， measurements should track how employees initially react to training materials the most valuable information to be gained is how well the material presented is being received。

 which is important feedback for trainers and presenters。

Learning is a second level where measurements should aim to determine how well participants are learning the material presented during the training program。

 one popular way to measure how much participants are learning is to issue pretest and post tests to compare employee performance on those exams。

The third level is behavior in this level， measurements are used to assess job performance after training。

 These evaluations focus on performance six weeks to six months after training and aim to determine whether the skills imparted during training are being used on the job。

 These measurements are based on observations， interviews， tests and surveys。 Finally。

 the fourth level of the Kirkpactic model is results。Feedback from result evaluations is gathered。

 which is the most meaningful aspect Training programs require investments。

 so employers need training programs to deliver results。

A result evaluation aims to measure whether there are tangible benefits to the organization's objectives from the training program。

 Here is an example of how this model works。 Connectives management wants to evaluate how effective a training program is for remote workers on using the organization's communication tools。

 Remember that the Kirkpat model as an evaluation tool for training。

 so it is implemented after the training program has begun。



![](img/1a2140ef8238c5a639d8d253c0077b45_4.png)

The trainers use Kirkpatrick's level 1 to evaluate the learners's reactions to the training program。

 They asked the learners to complete a survey to gauge their satisfaction with the training program。

 The survey includes questions such as， did you find the training program useful。

 Did the training program meet your expectations。 Would you recommend the training program to your colleagues。

Based on the survey results， the trainers found that the learners were highly satisfied with the training program and could assess the next level。

 Next， the trainer used Kirkpatrick's level 2 to evaluate how much the learners have gained in knowledge。

 skills and attitudes They conduct a pretest and post test to assess the learner's knowledge of communication tools offered by connective。

The trainers found that the learner score on the post test were significantly higher than the pretest。

 The trainers then used Kirkpatrick's levelve 3 to evaluate the extent to which the learners have applied what they have learned in their work。

 They conducted a follow up survey to assess whether the learners have used the communication tools they learned about in the training when working remotely with other team members。

Based on the survey's results， the trainers find that the learners have applied the communication tool they learned in their work。

Finally， the trainers use the Kirkpatrick's level4 to evaluate the impact of training program on the organization's goals Through analysis they determine whether the training program has positively impacted organization's performance efficiency based on the analysis。

 the trainers find that the training program has positively impacted the organization's performance efficiency due to the increased use of communication tools for remote employees。
