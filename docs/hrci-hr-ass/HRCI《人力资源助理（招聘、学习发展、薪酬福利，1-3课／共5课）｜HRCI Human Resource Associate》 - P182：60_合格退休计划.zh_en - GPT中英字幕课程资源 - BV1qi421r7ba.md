Qualified retirement plans provide tax benefits for both employees and employers guidelines for these plans are outlined by theEmployee Retirement Income Security Act or Arerisa。

Qualified plans must be available to all employees in an organization。

 and additional benefits cannot be offered to shareholders， executives。

 supervisors or highly compensated employees。 Nonqualified retirement plans don't receive the same tax benefits as qualified plans。

 This difference is because the benefits of nonqualified plans exceed the limits placed on qualified plans。

 If an organization offers non-qualified retirement plans。

 It is not necessary that the plan extends to all employees。

 This means certain employees might receive benefits that others do not。



![](img/ec36a675fde780e7ee67a8545f86142d_4.png)

Employees are not immediately entitled to the funds that employers contribute to their pension plans。

 the point at which employees are entitled to these funds is called vesting。



![](img/ec36a675fde780e7ee67a8545f86142d_6.png)

Vesting applies whether or not the employer remains employed with the organization。



![](img/ec36a675fde780e7ee67a8545f86142d_8.png)

According to your visa guidelines， employer contributions are 20% vested after three years of service。

 vesting increases incrementally until seven years of service。

 at which point contributions are 100% vested。 Employ contributions are always 100% vested。

 meaning employees are always entitled to the contributions they make to their pension plans。
