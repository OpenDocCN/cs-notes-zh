Experiments involve two types of variables， independent， independent。

 the independent variable is the possible cause of change in the dependent variables。

 which are affected by the independent treatment。Experiments typically involve at least two groups。

 the experimental group exposed to the independent variable and the control group with no exposure to the independent variable。

For example， the HR team at Urban Attire wants to evaluate the effectiveness of a new leadership development training program。

They randomly assign a group of employees to either the experimental group。

 those who receive the training， or the control group， those who do not receive the training。

Both groups complete a pre training assessment to establish a baseline level of leadership skills。

The treatment group receives leadership development training while the control group continues with their regular duties。



![](img/5eff0462435e75ed01bedfdb9e1d5887_8.png)

After the training program is complete， both groups take a post training assessment to measure their level of leadership skills。

 The organization compares the changes in leadership skills between the treatment and control groups to determine whether the training program is effective。

Randomly assigning participants to the treatment and control groups helps to ensure that any difference in outcomes between the two groups are due to the training intervention and not to other factors。

This type of experimental design can provide a strong evidence of the effectiveness of a training program。
