In 1954， Peter Druckcker introduced Management by Objects or MBO。

 a collaborative framework that enables organizations to set and achieve their objectives in a disciplined manner by creating effective communication and alignment between employees and managers。

 MBO enhances an organization's performance and drive success In this video。

 we will explore the MBO process from setting organizational objectives to rewarding employee achievements。

The MBO process consists of four steps designed to connect employees and their managers。

 enabling them to set clear and measurable objectives or goals Let's review each step in more detail。

😊。

![](img/e6fb81e6c4b99d4c46ecf6994d5e9c75_2.png)

Collaborative objective setting leads to more realistic。

 challenging objectives and increased engagement。 Each objective the team sets must adhere to the smart criteria。

 Smart objectives or goals are specific， measurable， attainable， realistic and time bound。

 applying these criteria helps managers develop well definedfined business objectives and create a clear plan。

 which they are more likely to achieve。😊，For example。

 make better sales calls is not a smart objective to make this smart。

 the objective should be phrased as sales department meets weekly to improve sales call practices。



![](img/e6fb81e6c4b99d4c46ecf6994d5e9c75_4.png)

There are three types of objectives in the MBO process routine objectives include ongoing tasks that establish a level of quality。

 In objectives and improvement objectives include new challenges。

 while innovative objectives often receive more focus。

 reaching routine objectives is necessary for maintaining consistent quality。



![](img/e6fb81e6c4b99d4c46ecf6994d5e9c75_6.png)

Once the manager and employee sets the objectives the next step is to create an action plan that outlines the steps needed to achieve them These action plans are developed at the individual。

 department or organizational level， depending on the scope of the objectives。😊，For example。

 an individual action plan may include tasks like conducting market research and developing sales strategies。

 a departmental action plan could involve improving customer service training at the organizational level action plans coordinate efforts across departments。

By providing a clear roadmap for success， action plans give employees a solid understanding of their objectives and the necessary actions to accomplish them in the next step。

 a manager and employee meet periodically to review the employee's progress toward achieving their objectives the meetings allow the manager to provide feedback on the employee's performance。

 highlighting areas where they perform well and areas that may need improvement。😊。

Objectives are also updated or revised during the meetings to adapt to any changes in the business environment for example。

 if an employee aims to increase customer satisfaction。

 the manager may provide feedback on how the employee can improve their communication with customers。

😊。

![](img/e6fb81e6c4b99d4c46ecf6994d5e9c75_8.png)

If there is a shift in customer preferences or market conditions。

 the objective may need to be revised to align with the new circumstances。

 ensuring the employee's efforts remain relevant and effective。



![](img/e6fb81e6c4b99d4c46ecf6994d5e9c75_10.png)

After setting objectives and creating action plans， a manager evaluates the employee's performance。

This assessment helps determine the extent to which the employee has successfully achieved their objectives。

Based on this evaluation， the manager rewards successful employees with bonuses。

 additional compensation， or other forms of recognition。

 these rewards motivate employees to reinforce their commitment to meeting objectives within the MBO framework。

😊。

 accountable and motivated through action planning， progress review and performance assessment。😊。
