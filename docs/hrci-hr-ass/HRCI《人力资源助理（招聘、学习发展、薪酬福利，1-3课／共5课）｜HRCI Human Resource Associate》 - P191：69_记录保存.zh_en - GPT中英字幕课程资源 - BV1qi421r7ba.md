According to the Fair Labor Standard Act or the FLSA。

 employees are required to keep physical or digital employee records with personal information。

 as well as information relating to pay and pay periods personal information includes records of names。

 addresses， birth dates， Social Security numbers and occupation for each employee。

Employers must also keep records relating to pay and pay periods。

 This documentation includes how many hours an employee worked each day during the period。

 and the time work began and ended。 recordss must also include regular pay accrued during the period and any incentive pay that was issued。

Likewise， employers must keep information relating to hiring efforts。

 including applications and employment tests， as well as terminations。😊。

Because this documentation is sensitive and confidential， physical records should be burned。

 shredded or pulverized so they cannot be restored or reconstructed。

 Digital records should also be erased or reformatted so they cannot be reconstructed or read。

Federal regulations define how long records must be kept。
