Businesses with more than 10 employees are required by OSHA to keep complete and accurate records of work related illnesses and injuries。

Industries that are identified as low risk， such as motor vehicle dealers， gasoline stations。

 shoe stores， florists， software publishers， and many more are partially exempt from this requirement。

Employers are also required to notify OSHA of certain work related events， for example。

 employers must notify OSHA within eight hours of a fatality if an employee is killed on a job。

If an employee is injured and requires hospitalization， amputation， or loses an eye working。

 employers must notify OSHA within 24 hours。If an organization fails to comply with this law。

 they face significant penalties and fines Let's examine common OSHA forms that you will likely encounter Form 300 also known as the log of work related injuries or illnesses is used by employers to report work related injuries and illness The form requires information about the type of injury or illness。

 how it was caused， identifying information about the individual who was injured or became ill。

 and where and when the injury or illness took place This form must be completed within one week of the employer becoming aware of the incident Form 300 a is a form that summarizes the illnesses and injuries that occur on the work site during each work year。

Form 301 requires employers to provide information about where an injured employee was treated for an illness or injury。

 the name of the treating physician， detailed information about the employee's actions prior to the incident and information about the cause of the incident。

Employers are not required to keep records of nonwork related incidents an incident is considered nonwork related if it was the result of activities that took place outside the workplace。

 it became apparent at the workplace， but the employee was not in the workplace to perform work。

 it occurred during a vehicular accident on company property while the employee was commuting to or from work。

 it resulted from medication the employee was taking for a non work related illness。
