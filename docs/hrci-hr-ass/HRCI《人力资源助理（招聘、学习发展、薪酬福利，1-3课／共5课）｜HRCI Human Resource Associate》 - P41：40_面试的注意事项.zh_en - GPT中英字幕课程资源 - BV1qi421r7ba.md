When interviewing a candidate for your organization。

 you want to ensure that they are the best fit possible。

 You want to maximize the value of your questions。 You should also avoid questions that at best do not help you effectively differentiate candidates and at worst。

 violate the law In this video， we'll examine a few interviewing a do's and don'ts。

 Let's start with what you should do。 you should always ask only those questions that are pertinent to the position you are trying to fill Que should be related to require tasks or skills for the role。

 For example， if the opening is for a social media marketer position， you might ask the candidate。

 what is your favorite social media platform and why But you wouldn't ask that question of someone interviewing for a data analyst position。

😊。

![](img/de0096aba361acf3d672704440eb7f20_2.png)

You should also ask each candidate the same questions or questions that cover the same topic。

 This technique makes it easier to compare candidate' responses。 and now what you should not do。

When interviewing， you should avoid asking questions that are personal in nature or unrelated to the job qualifications。

 For example， you should not ask a candidate where they were born。

 This type of questions does not address how the candidate might perform in the job。

 It may also introduce unintended bias towards or against the candidate。

 You can avoid many kinds of bias by keeping to questions related to the candidate's competency in the role。

 Later in this course will explore different types of interview bias and how to avoid them。



![](img/de0096aba361acf3d672704440eb7f20_4.png)

You must also avoid asking questions about protected classes like race， religion。

 age or marital status。 Not only are these personal attributes irrelevant to a person's ability to perform a function。

 but they are also protected by law。 It is illegal to ask about these attributes。

 and doing so may cause discrimination during the candidate selection process。

 Let's explore a quick comparison of inappropriate versus appropriate interview questions。

 an inappropriate question would be where were you born Instead， you can ask， if you are hired。

 can you provide proof that you are authorized to work in the United States。

 This question is relevant。 Instead of， are you disabled， you can ask。

 are you able to perform the functions that are essential to the job with reasonable accommodations。

😊，Instead of， are you religious and if you are， are you willing to work on Sundays， you should ask。

 this job will require that employees work on weekends。 Are you able to do so。

Each of these reframed questions is relevant to the functions of the job。

 they will not introduce bias or violate the laws protecting against discrimination。😊。

 Ask all candidates the same questions and don't ask personal questions about protected classes。
