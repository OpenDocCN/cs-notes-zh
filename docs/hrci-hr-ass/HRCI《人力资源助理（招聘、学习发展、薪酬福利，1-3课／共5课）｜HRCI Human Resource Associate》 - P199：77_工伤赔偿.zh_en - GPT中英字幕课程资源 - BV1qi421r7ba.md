Now that you've learned about claims， we'll explore some of the specific types of claims you'll need to handle for your organization and employees。

The first kind of claim we're going to cover is workers' compensation claims。

 Workers' compensation is a type of insurance that is allotted to employees to cover injuries or illness from their workplace。

 Workers compensation is typically funded through state agency or private insurance and can provide wage replacement and medical benefits to qualifying employees。



![](img/9b3c5adbbcf75387bc075bb2b9f31f06_2.png)

It is important to note if an employee is awarded workers compensation they give up their right to sue the organization for negligence Work' compensation can cover a range of expenses and activities coverage includes medical bills。

 wage recovery， reimbursement for medical related expenses and possibly benefits to the dependence of employees killed on the job。

When processing a worker's compensation claim， it is imperative to be accurate， timely。

 and as fair as possible。

First， you should record a statement of events from the employee。

 This should occur after the employee has been seen by a medical professional to address any urgent needs。

 The statement of events should include the type of injury， where the injury is on the body。

 the date， time and place where the injury happened。 and whether there were other employees involved。

 You should also record witness statements to gain additional information。

 be sure to gather details about the timeline of events before， during and after the incident。

 as well as the extent of any medical treatment provided。 Finally。

 you should know any laws that apply， such as the Americans with disabilities Act。

 Once you have documented all the pertinent information。

 you will file the claim with both your organization's insurance company and the state's workers compensation agency。

 You must meet your state's deadlines for reporting and filing a work' compensation claim to avoid any issues。

 These deadlines vary by state。 Next， the state agency will decide whether or not to investigate the incident。

Alternatively， your organization can choose not to file the claim on the employee's behalf。
