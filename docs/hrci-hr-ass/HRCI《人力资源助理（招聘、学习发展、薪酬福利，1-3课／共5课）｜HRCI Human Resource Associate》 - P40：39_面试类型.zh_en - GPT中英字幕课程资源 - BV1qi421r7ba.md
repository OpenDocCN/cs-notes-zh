Now that you've been introduced to the interview process。

 let's go over a few different types of interviews you can use。

Each interview type has its own strengths， which are important to understand so that you can choose the most effective type for your goals。

😊。

![](img/82f52993493cf3d65ad59ca2a617a8b1_2.png)

The first type is the group interview In a group interview。

 multiple candidates are interviewed at the same time。

 This type is useful to directly compare candidates answers to the same question。

 group interviews also permit you to observe how interviewees interact with one another。

 These observations can provide insight into how well applicants work with other people。

 Next is the panel or board interview。 In a panel interview。

 several interviewers participate at the same time。 All interviewers on the panel may ask questions。

 or they may be a lead interviewer who ask most of the questions while the other panel members observe。

😊，Sometimes you need to know how candidates would react to a particular scenario Beal interviews are helpful in this instance During behavioral interviews。

 candidates are asked to explain how they responded to problems or events in the past。

 The assumption is that these past behaviors will predict how employees would respond to similar situations in the future。

 The problems and events that you focus on during the interview should relate to specific and important aspects of the position。

😊，While behavioral interviews focus on an employee's prior behavior。

 hypothetical interviews ask candidates how they would respond to imagined situations in the future。

 these are also called situational interviews Another type of interviews are patterned interviews patterned interviews approach the interview by topics applicants may be asked different questions。

 but all address the same topics。😊。

![](img/82f52993493cf3d65ad59ca2a617a8b1_4.png)

We have a few more interview types to review。😊，Directive interviews are rigid。

 An interview director asks candidates a pre establishedlish set of questions。

 These may also be called structured interviews。 non directive interviews are less formal than directive interviews。

 As the interviewer， you ask broad， open ended questions。

 Open ended questions allow a candidate to guide the conversation。

 This interview can provide a great deal of insight into a candidate's character and personality。

 but because each conversation may cover different issues and topics。

 It is difficult to evaluate candidates consistently。😊。

Semi structured interviews find a balance between directive and non directive approaches。

 You prepare some key questions in advance， but are also free to ask follow up questions or explore different issues and topics depending on how the candidate responds to the questions。

Lastly， we have stress interviews which place candidates in anxiety and deing situations to see how they react。

 These interviews are most useful when you are evaluating candidates who will regularly encounter stressful situations while on the job。

😊，The needs of your organization and the roles you are trying to fill will require different approaches to get the right information。

You now have a good introduction to the different types of interviews and how you can use them to find the best candidate for your organization。

 Later in this lesson， we'll examine strategies for conducting a good interview。

 as well as things to avoid。😊。
