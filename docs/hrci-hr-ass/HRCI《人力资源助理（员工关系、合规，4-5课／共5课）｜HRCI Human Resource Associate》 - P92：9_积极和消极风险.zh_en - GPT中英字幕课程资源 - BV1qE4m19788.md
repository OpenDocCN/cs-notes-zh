When people think of risk， they often think of something negative。

 such as a natural disaster or employee injury。 However。

 not all risks are bad and some can even be beneficial。



![](img/6f843890c7e1e0eba10041fd667d3977_2.png)

In this video， you will learn about positive and negative risk。

A positive risk is an opportunity that has the potential to bring success to an organization。

 It can offer the possibility of creating value， gaining advantages， or achieving opportunities。😊。

If this type of risk occurs， it can have a positive impact on objectives such as innovation。

 cost and time savings， enhanced performance， competitive advantage and more。 For example。

 Connective has recently experienced high demand for reliable user friendly video calling systems。



![](img/6f843890c7e1e0eba10041fd667d3977_4.png)

As a result， they invest heavily in a new software security program to prevent data breaches。

This investment is a positive risk because it has the potential to protect connective against hackers and others who could try to do the organization harm。

Incorporating new software always carries risk， such as technology glitches or training that affects employee productivity。

 but it can also be beneficial。 Connective realizes that this software has several potential advantages。

 including lower legal fees， reduced labor costs， reputation protection and more。

These advantages outweigh the negative risks， and connective is pleased with their choice。

 A negative risk is known as a threat that has the ability to cause harm。

 Negative risks are found in an organization's daily operations and are often easily noticeable。

 and negative risk can impact a project organization or individual and lead to losses， damages。

 failures or other undesirable outcomes to continue with the example because connect invest in the new software。

 They decide to forego， adding a password feature for users to secure their video calls。😊。



![](img/6f843890c7e1e0eba10041fd667d3977_6.png)

![](img/6f843890c7e1e0eba10041fd667d3977_7.png)

They do not think this feature is necessary due to the security system's strength。

 Not requiring a password is a negative risk， because even though the software is strong。

 technology is constantly changing。 In the future， hackers may be able to breach the software。

 which could damage connect's reputation， create financial lawsuit due to legal fees。

 reduce productivity and more。

![](img/6f843890c7e1e0eba10041fd667d3977_9.png)

When managing risk， it is essential to consider both positive and negative risks。

 as they can both support balanced decision making and maximize the chance of organizational success。

![](img/6f843890c7e1e0eba10041fd667d3977_12.png)
