Common law is the body of law established by judicial decisions；

 It is distinct from but equally important to the body of law established by legislation passed by Congress and regulations issued by the federal government's executive branch。

Violating common law doctrines may result in legal action， often through torts。

 A tort is a legal action taken by an individual who feels wronged by another individual's failure to meet an obligation。

This obligation may be established by common law doctrine or a voluntary obligation entered into through a contract。



![](img/71987265cadfb680a358d1c8aed63cec_4.png)

Let's discuss a few examples to illustrate how common law doctrines function within an organization。



![](img/71987265cadfb680a358d1c8aed63cec_6.png)

Constructive discharge occurs when an employer creates a hostile and unpleasant workplace that leads an employee to quit。

In many states， former employees have the right to file a lawsuit when this happens。

 although they may be required to provide varying levels of evidence。

Some states demand that employees demonstrate their employer's intention to force their resignation。

 while others require employees to show the extent of the negative workplace conditions。For example。

 cutting a worker's hours or pay might lead to negative working conditions and therefore could be an example of constructive discharge。

Damation is a legal concept that protects an individual against accusations or other statements that may damage their reputation。

 Organs must be cautious about making statements that could harm someone's reputation in the community or hinder an employee。

 even if terminated from finding another job。This means that employers should avoid saying anything untrue or irrelevant to an employee's work performance。

For example， in response to a reference request， suppose an employer shares information that is either false or irrelevant to how well an employee performed in their job。

In this case， the employer may be legally accountable for any damages that occurred as a result of their actions。

Communications that are job related， unequivocal， specific， and accurate， are protected。

Employers can protect themselves when writing references by providing only detailed information and obtaining written authorization for references from the relevant employee or former employee。

Employment at will means an employer can terminate an employee at any time without giving a reason or prior notice。

 Likewise， employees have the freedom to leave their job without having to provide notice。

 Suppose an organization hires a bookkeeper under an out will employment arrangement。

 A few months later， the organization is forced to downsize due to financial difficulties。

Under the at will employment arrangement， the organization holds the legal right to terminate the employee at any time。

 As a result， they terminate the employee without providing a reason or advanced notice。

 Although at will employment is a default arrangement for employees without a contract。

 federal statutes and judicial decisions in the past 60 years have significantly restricted the power of employers to terminate at will employees。

 Promissory Estopple is common law protection that may require an employer to  fulfill promises to an employee in certain situations。

Employers may be required to keep promises to employees when the commitment is clear。

 specific and reasonable。

![](img/71987265cadfb680a358d1c8aed63cec_8.png)

An example of a promissory isstoppo might be applied when an employer promises an employee to pay them a specific monthly amount for the duration of their employment。



![](img/71987265cadfb680a358d1c8aed63cec_10.png)

Responddent superior comes from Latin and means let the master answer。

 It is a common law protection that holds an employer responsible for any negligence or wrongdoing of their employees when they are acting on behalf of or are performing tasks for the employer。

For example， suppose a driver working for a delivery service causes an accident due to negligence。

 in that case， the injured party may have the right to file a lawsuit against a delivery service。



![](img/71987265cadfb680a358d1c8aed63cec_12.png)

In some cases， an employer may be responsible for any civil offenses committed by an employee。

 even if the employer was unaware of the employee's actions。
