Flexible work arrangements often allow employees to customize their work hours and days。

 while flexible schedules can include the opportunity to work less than a whole week。

 many organizations require employees to work full time to qualify for these options。

Organizations that offer flexible schedules often require that employees are present during core hours。

 where managers can work and communicate with all team members。

Flexible hours are similar to flexible work weekss in which employees work longer shifts in exchange for working fewer days。

For example， some employees may work 10 hour shifts Monday through Thursday in the summer to have Fridays off。

Some examples of flexible hours include staggered hours in which employees have different start and stop times instead of a traditional 9 am to 5 pm shift。

 depending on the policy， these hours can vary throughout the week or remain consistent through a fixed schedule for example。

 one employee works from 5 amm to 1 pm another from 7 amm to 3 pm and another from 11 amm to 7 pm。

Split shifts are another option in split shifts， employees take a longer scheduled break than usual。

 often in the afternoon and make up the extra time by starting work earlier or staying later The last type is variable days。

 This is when an organization requires full- time employees to work 40 hours per week。 However。

 they can work a different number of hours each day。 For example。

 an employee works 10 hours on Monday，6 hours on Tuesday and so on。

 The next flexible work strategies telecommuting and remote work。

Telecommuting， along with remote work， allows organizations to recruit and hire beyond geographic barriers and access a wider pool of candidates。

Remote work is when employees can work from home or anywhere offsite In some cases。

 remote work benefits for employees include work life balance， lack of commute。

 freedom to live in different locations， reduce transport costs， and a positive environmental impact。
