A value statement is a statement issued by an organization's leadership that indicates how business should be conducted within that organization。

These values are principles that guide all of an organization's actions。

Common corporate values can include integrity， respect， product and service excellence， and teamwork。

First， it is important to begin by brainstorming potential values。

 create a comprehensive list of current and potential values by asking employees what they think the organization's core values are and what values they hope to see in the future。

Next， review the list of values Group similar values and eliminate any that you feel do not represent your organization or share too much overlap with other groups After you have grouped your values。

 determine which are important to the success of the company。

 Be sure the number of values you choose is manageable。

 The next step is to draft a value statement by using the final set of values you have established。

 These are typically bullet points or numbered lists。

 Ref the list to short a succinct language that can easily convey the value。😊。

Once the statement is drafted， review it with leadership。

 employees and customers or clients it's a good idea to ask for feedback and use their input to revise and finalize your statement。

😊，It is essential to communicate the organization's values to employees and customers you might achieve this by offering training for employees。

 publishing the values on the organization's website。

 or posting them in common areas around the workplace。😊，Finally。

 integrate the values into everyday business practices and procedures。

 ensure they are widely available for review and reference。
