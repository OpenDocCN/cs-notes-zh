Organizations should track the cost of benefits to determine which benefits justify the expenses involved and which do not。

One method of tracking benefit costs is to look at the annual cost of making a particular benefit available。

 This is the annual cost method。 another way to track the cost of benefits is to measure the cost per employee per year to do so。

 divide the total cost of the benefit for the year by the number of employees who receive that benefit。

 This kind of analysis can help determine whether a certain benefits are being used by only a small portion of employees。



![](img/69e6c883d8ce0bbfcd4b58277c66f948_6.png)

For example， let's say a company pays $195，000 a year to provide health insurance to its employees。

 there are 25 employees at the company， dividing the total cost by the number of employees。
