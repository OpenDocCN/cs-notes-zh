The second level of the Kirkpat model evaluates how well the participants learn the new knowledge。

 skills， or attitudes through pretests before the lesson and post tests once the lesson is complete。

The third level of their Kirkpatrick model evaluates how well the participants apply the knowledge and skills learned during the training program。

Urban entiretire employees are asked to complete a self assessment survey six months after the training program to measure how well they had applied the new knowledge and skills in their work。

The results showed that employees had applied the knowledge and skills and that there was a noticeable improvement in their leadership and management practices。

The fourth level of the Kirkpatrick model evaluates the impact of the training program on the organization's performance。

Performance indicators such as employee engagement and productivity were measured six months after the training program to evaluate its impact on urbanr attire's performance。
