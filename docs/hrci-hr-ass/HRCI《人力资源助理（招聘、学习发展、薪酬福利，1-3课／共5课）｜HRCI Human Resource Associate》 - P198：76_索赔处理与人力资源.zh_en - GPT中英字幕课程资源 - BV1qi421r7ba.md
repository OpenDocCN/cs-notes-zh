Processing various claims is an integral part of HR tasks and compensation and benefits related claims are the most common type。

 This video will provide an introduction to claims processing and how it might apply to your future HR role。

Every organization is different， but generally all employee related claims should be brought to HR。

 and HR rep will document the claim according to organization standards and legal requirements。

There are various types of compensation and benefits related claims that the HR department must be equipped to handle。



![](img/5c3bb01516e7dec1cc77cf1416599828_2.png)

Let's explore the most common kinds of compensation and benefit claims。

Workers compensation is a type of insurance that is allotted to employees who are injured at or get an illness from their workplace。

 Wage and hour claims are usually associated with violations of the Fair Labor Standards Act or F LSA Disability benefits are awarded to individuals who suffer from an injury or illness that is unrelated to work。

 Family and medical Leave Act or F MLA violations can occur when an organization denies leave to an employee。



![](img/5c3bb01516e7dec1cc77cf1416599828_4.png)

There are two slightly less common types of claims you might experience。

 these are CObra claims for continued health coverage under a previous policy or OSHA claims regarding workplace safety or hazards。

These claims are processed differently， but you should handle them with the same thoroughness and attention to detail。

![](img/5c3bb01516e7dec1cc77cf1416599828_7.png)

It's important for your organization to handle these employee claims with care and precision。

 These actions help employees feel safe， cared for and respected。

 Care and precision can also avoid mistakes during the claims process and reduce your organization's risk of penalties。
