 but how does an HR team figure out what their employees need？In this video。

![](img/ecf9072e7f668468474b5078c3306852_2.png)

A training needs analysis or TNA is the method used to identify specific training goals for employees by examining areas in the organization that could be improved。

 this exploration usually begins with an understanding of the core competency needed by an organization it can use questions like what tasks are required of an employee or what knowledge skills and abilities will an employee need to assess in order to succeed in their role。

The needs assessment should surface whatever gaps exist between the current employee skill sets and those required for either a particular role or the organization as a whole。



![](img/ecf9072e7f668468474b5078c3306852_4.png)

There are a few approaches you can take， depending on how broad you want your analysis to be。

 let's use urban attire as an example。

![](img/ecf9072e7f668468474b5078c3306852_6.png)

A training needs analysis can be used to identify organizationwide training needs， for instance。

 the human resources team at Urban Attire may conduct an analysis and find that employees lack consistent training on a new product such as a packable jacket。

They can then provide training resources to their relevant stores or individuals。

Another approach for training needs analysis is to conduct them at the department level to identify training needs or support needs。

 for instance， human resources at urban attire might conduct one with the suit and Txedo department and identify the need for refresher training in sufficient staffing during prom season。

HTR teams can also conduct very narrow TNAs to examine individual employees gaps in their skills for their job。

For example， because Clara has worked in a bookstore but not a clothing store。

 her T showed that she needed some extra training on the various apparel that urban attire sells。

 but that her customer service skills were excellent。



![](img/ecf9072e7f668468474b5078c3306852_8.png)

TNAs can be extremely useful in getting to know not just individual employees needs。

 but those of a broader group as well let's compare the two scopes a TA can take。



![](img/ecf9072e7f668468474b5078c3306852_10.png)

An organizational analysis examines how systems within an organization function。

 including workforce planning， product quality and an organization's culture。

 it involves quantifying outcomes and identifying variables that influence them。



![](img/ecf9072e7f668468474b5078c3306852_12.png)

An individual analysis focuses on the performance of particular employees the goal should be to identify employees who need additional skills or abilities to perform well in their jobs。

Training should be tailored to meet those needs and employees who don't need training should not be required to participate。
