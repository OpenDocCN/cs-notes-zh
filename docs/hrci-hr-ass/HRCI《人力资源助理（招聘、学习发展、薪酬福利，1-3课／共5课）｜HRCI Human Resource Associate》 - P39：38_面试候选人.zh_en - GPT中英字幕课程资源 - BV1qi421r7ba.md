Now that you understand the screening process， let's go over the next step in the selection process。

 interviewing candidates interviewing helps you take the list of candidates who meet the requirements of the position identified through the screening process and find the best candidates job interviews are used to probe for more information that is related to the job and are useful for assessing the applicant's motivation and interpersonal skills。

😊。

![](img/f1ac93067ff0203051c15f610e4cd99f_2.png)

It is also a chance for the applicant to determine whether the job is an appropriate fit for them。

During job interviews， employers should try to discover as much as possible about the applicants under consideration。

 One way to accomplish this is by asking open ended questions and avoiding questions that have obvious or simple responses。

😊，The questions you ask are best when they are related to specific parts of the role the candidate is expected to fill。

 For example， if an employee will need leadership skills for a role。

 you can ask something like when was a time you had to be a leader。

 Remember when structuring an interview that you will want to compare candidates later。

 Ask the same questions to candidates or questions that cover the same topic to make it easier to compare their answers to each other。

 avoiding questions that don't cover the scope of the position will help limit bias against candidates。

 Other potential pitfalls to avoid in interviews include asking questions about protected categories like race。

 religion or marital status。 Getting the right candidate is a difficult process。

 but by asking the right questions， you can establish which candidate will best fit the role in your organization。

😊。
