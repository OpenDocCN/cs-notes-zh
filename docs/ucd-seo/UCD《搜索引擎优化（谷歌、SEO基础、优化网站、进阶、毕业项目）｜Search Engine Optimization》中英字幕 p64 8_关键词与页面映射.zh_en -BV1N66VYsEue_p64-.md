How do you keep it all organized as you choose which keywords to use on each page？In this lesson。

 we'll discuss how to build a keyword map and how to determine the best use of keywords within your site。

A keyword map is basically a document which outlines which keywords belong to which pages of your site。

You aren't using keywords on your website so much as you are using certain keywords on pages of your website。

This has a few purposes。First， it allows us to make sure we are creating focused pages around a particular keyword or group of keywords。

Instead of thinking about how individual keywords might perform。

Think about which keywords relate to a single topic to determine the theme of that page。

This allows us to strategically think about how we want to optimize the site as a whole。 Second。

 it ensures that we are not inadvertently cannibalizing our own efforts by using duplicated keywords or keywords that are too similar on more than one page。

This will cause those pages to compete with one another for the same search query。Third。

 it provides us with a document to reference as we are making recommendations and changes to the site。

This will help guide our efforts throughout the optimization process。Fourth。

 it is a great document clients can use as a reference once we are no longer working with them。

If they want to change or add content to a page later on。

They will be able to reference this document and easily see what the keyword focus of that page is。

This also helps them avoid the common mistake of reusing existing keywords later on。And fifth。

 but not least， this allows us to more easily gain insight into how our pages are performing for their keyword focus and topic。

Using Google Analytics， we can get an idea of how well these keywords are actually sitting in traffic by looking at organic landing pages and comparing those pages to our keyword map。

While you may not be receiving traffic for these exact terms。

 chances are likely you are receiving traffic for similar phrases。

You can then look at things like engagement level metrics and bounce rate to determine how well that page may be meeting the needs of your visitors。



![](img/0d76ee925d0e0aff1f03ebe5551ffeb9_5.png)

Remember， optimizing specific pages around a chosen set of keywords isn't about trying to trick Google into ranking your page higher。

It's about how well you can match the topic and language of a page to the intent of your user base。

The goal of optimizing pages in this fashion is to help search engines better understand your site and which pages are most relevant to a query。

This also helps you plan out your website architecture by seeing how different pages are related to one another。
