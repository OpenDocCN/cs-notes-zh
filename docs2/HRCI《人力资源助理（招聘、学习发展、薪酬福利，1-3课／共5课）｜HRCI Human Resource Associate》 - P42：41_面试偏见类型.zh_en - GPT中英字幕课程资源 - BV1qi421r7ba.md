Interviewing can be a very personal process。 During the interview phase。

 managers are vulnerable to making common judgment errors or being affected by bias。

 There are many kinds of unconscious bias that may not be obvious。

 The halo effect occurs when an interviewer judges an applicant on only one trait and allows that trait to influence their judgment of other unrelated traits。

 For example， you may assume that because someone who is well spoken with a large vocabulary will also be a talented engineer。

 Similarlyly， stereotyping occurs when a manager judges an applicant based on membership in a group。

 Although the applicant may or may not share the group's values or characteristics。 For example。

 older generations are not tech savvy and are resistant to change。

The central tendency bias is when the interviewer ratess all candidates with a similar score。

 often in the middle of the scale。😊，The interviewer might not want to grade a candidate too low and be hesitant to grade someone else too high。

 The result is each candidate ends with similar scores。

 even though there is a gap in qualifications for the position。

Contrast effect error occurs when candidates are compared to a single candidate。

If the single candidate is a weak fit， the rest of the candidates might all seem strong by comparison。

 For example， if the first applicant lacks the ability to speak a second language。

 the following candidates might appear stronger， despite the fact that they lack other desired traits。

Cultural noise occurs when a candidate attempts to respond to the interviewer's questions based on what they think the interviewer wants to hear or is socially acceptable。

😊，This response is often in place of a factual answer。

 and the interviewer doesn't recognize that the answer is not genuine。

An example of cultural noise is when a candidate tells the interviewer they prefer to work as part of a team when really they are most productive when they work independently。

😊，The candidate infers that working within a team is a more socially acceptable response。

Affinity bias is the tendency to communicate and rate highly those who have similar appearances。

 beliefs， experiences and backgrounds。 These similarities may include a shared cultural heritage or fondness for a particular extracurricular activity like rock climbing。

 Conm bias occurs when the interviewer has a preconceived notion of a result。

 and then seeks information that will corroborate or confirm the judgment。 For instance。

 the interviewer may assume someone with a particular accent may lack determination or focus。

 They may then ask leading questions about completing a difficult task。

 hoping the candidate will suggest cutting corners。😊。

Nonverbal bias occurs when an interviewer is influenced by body language， appearance， or eye contact。

😊，An applicant whose leg bounces up and down during an interview may lead the interviewer to assume the candidate is anxious instead of excited。

 There are many biases that can affect you。 Despite your best intentions。

 it is important to be aware of these unconscious biases， before you begin the selection process。

 This will ensure your choice reflects the best candidate for the role。 Next。

 you'll learn about best practices to create interview panels to avoid bias during the selection process。
