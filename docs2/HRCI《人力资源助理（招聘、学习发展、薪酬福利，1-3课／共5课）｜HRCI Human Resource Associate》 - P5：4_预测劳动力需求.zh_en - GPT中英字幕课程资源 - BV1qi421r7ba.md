One of the most important skills an HR professional can master is forecasting the human resource needs of an organization。

 The ability to take the organization's strategic goals and determine a specific number and type of jobs that need to be filled or eliminated is a critical tool in your HR tool set。

 But how do I actually do that。 Great question。 There are a number of methods to forecast。

 Let's start with a few ways to model and analyze organization needs。 The first model is unit demand。

 When using the unit demand method， you'll ask units or department managers to report on both the volume of business activity。

 As well as how many people are needed to carry out that activity。😊。



![](img/8a5941975555c34f658b88b5154953e5_2.png)

It's important to gather this information from several unit managers to get a clear picture for the entire organization。

You can then combine the feedback from all units to determine the organization's staffing needs in the coming years。

 Another approach is trend projections。 T projections， predict future employment needs。

 The projections are based on expectations of how particular employment related factors may change over time。

 For instance， if you need salespeople to sell your product and sales are projected to increase。

 You're likely to need more salespeople in the future。😊。

To understand how these trends align with employment needs。

 you'll need to have a good awareness of the factors that affect staffing in your organization。

 For instance， what happens when sales increase or production drops？

Reviewing the organization's past performance and how these factors corresponded to person hour needs is a great way to start。

 similar to trend projections， you can use our probabilistic model to gain insight as well。

 Probabilistic models make predictions about future conditions using computer simulations。

 These models consider possible outcomes based on proposed economic conditions or organization shifts。

 The computer simulations used by probabilistic models have two potential limitations。 First。

 they are only as accurate as the information you provide。 And second。

 they can become costly and unwieldly as more variables are added for near term projections a workload analysis can be helpful。

 A workload analysis determines the output of productivity。

 the organization expects to achieve in the immediate future。

 Then employment needs are calculated in。😊。

This analysis is completed in three parts。First， you determine the kind of work needed to meet the productivity goals。

 Then you translate the work into person hours。 For instance。

 how much labor will these tasks require， and how long will they take。 Finally。

 the workload expectation can be translated into employment needs。

 We've got three more techniques to cover。 Let's move on to ratio analysis。

 a ratio analysis examines historical information about the workforce pertaining to a particular faucet of the organization。

 such as sales or production。 For example， in the past。

 a team needed a ratio of one project manager for every seven clients。 In the next quarter。

 the team anticipates the loss of one project manager and the addition of 14 new clients。

 using a ratio analysis。 You determine that the organization needs to hire three new project managers to meet its needs。
