Smart goals ensure that your goals are clear， specific and achievable enough to guide and motivate you effectively When creating goals。

 remember the acronym Smart， which stands for specific， Measurable， attainable。

 relevant and time bound Let's examine each piece of this acronym。😊。



![](img/e19896d1cafe550a34dc1cb5cebf84af_4.png)

First， goals should be specific。 You should state how you will know when a goal has been accomplished and how you might go about reaching it。

 Next， goals should be measurable， andmeasurable goal allows you to monitor your progress and makes it obvious when you have met it。

 Go should also be attainable。 attainable goals will keep you motivated。

 Unistic goals are demoralizing and waste time。 A goal should be relevant to your organization's long termm aspirations。

😊，Finally， a goal should be time bound， when identifying goals。

 be sure to use realistic time constraints。

![](img/e19896d1cafe550a34dc1cb5cebf84af_6.png)

Let's go over an example， RE， an HR professional from Urban Attire。

 learns that store managers need more training on handling conflict between team members。

They have already created a webinar style training around this topic and now need to figure out how to implement the training for all managers in all the urban attire stores across the country。

😊，Rri writes the following smart goal for implementing this training In four weeks。

 all managers in 25% of stores will complete the virtual training on conflict resolution in scheduled sessions。

 This goal is specific It aims to have managers in 25% of stores completing the training through scheduled training sessions。

 This provides clear direction for what needs to be accomplished。 The goal is measurable。

 It specifies a target of all managers at 25% of stores。

 This allows progress to be tracked and evaluated。 The goal is achievable。

 It focuses on scheduling and implementing training sessions for managers in the stores。

 The goal is relevant。 It has been previously identified that managers in stores are not adequately prepared to deal with conflict among team members。

 This new training will create a better working environment in all stores。 The goal。😊。

Now that RAE has created their goal， they can begin planning intermediate steps or things that need to be done in order to reach the goal。

One of these steps for Ari might look like working with regional managers to schedule training sessions in local stores。

When creating intermediate steps for your SM goal， it can be helpful to work backwards you can start with what needs to be done just before reaching your goal and then just before that and so on until you can determine what should be done right away。

Creating HR goals for your organization can be difficult because there are so many ways to start。

Using the SM goal method can help you to create attainable and relevant goals。😊。

Once your goal has been created， working backwards can help you to create a measurable timeline to achieve it。
