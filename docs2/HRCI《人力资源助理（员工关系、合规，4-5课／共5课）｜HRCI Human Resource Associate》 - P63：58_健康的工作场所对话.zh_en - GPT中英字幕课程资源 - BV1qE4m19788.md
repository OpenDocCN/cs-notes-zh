 whether they agree with them or not。Members consider it socially risky to raise objections and therefore avoid doing so in groupthink the desire for unanimity surpasses the expression of dissenting individual viewpoints successful creative teams embrace diversity。

The risk， however， is that differences might produce destructive rather than constructive conflict and impede effectiveness。

Still， experts agree that some level of conflict promotes the creative process in a Harvard Business Review article how management teams can have a good fight。

 Kathleen Eisenhart and colleagues found that technology firms with high levels of constructive。

 substantive conflict were the most creative。In fact。

 there are several elements that signal whether a team is healthy or dysfunctional。

A healthyy team's members are open to and respect each other's ideas， remain objective。

 focus their discussions on facts， and seek consensus around common goals。



![](img/dd533a54e571c13eafb7d3e847f78a15_8.png)

Meanwhile， a dysfunctional team's members won't acknowledge others ideas or even dismiss them outright。

 they make the discussion personal or respond emotionally， focus on opinions or personalities。

 and might even have factions within a team that seek victory of their own point of view。



![](img/dd533a54e571c13eafb7d3e847f78a15_10.png)

In order to prevent a team from becoming dysfunctional and help the conversation stay healthy and productive。

 a facilitator or team leader might be appointed。The task for the facilitator or team leader is to guide the team through substantive conflict while avoiding personal strife。

 It must be made clear to the group what kinds of comments are and are not permissible。

 The group also should be clear about how to challenge ideas， rather than people。

A team leader can employ a number of tactics to encourage a constructive debate it's helpful to identify differences within the team to achieve a common understanding of all perspectives。

😊，Team members can also paraphrase each other's ideas and invite constructive face toface feedback。

 Another way to prevent group think and encourage healthy workplace conversations is to empower all the members of the team。

 The effectiveness of the team depends in no small measure on the empowerment of its members。

 A group can harness its creativity to solve a problem when members feel confident they can challenge conventional wisdom。

 flout traditional thinking and even disregard the rules as they exist A team will be reluctant to embrace this mandate if its members aren't confident that they can think freely and express themselves openly without concern for censor or criticism。
