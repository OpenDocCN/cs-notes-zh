Given a probability P of an event occurring， we know that the amount of information that you get from being told that a particular event occurred is log base 2 of one over that probability。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_2.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_3.png)

Using that information， let's try to answer the following questions。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_5.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_6.png)

Some one picks a name out of a hat known to contain the names of five women and three men and tells you that a man has been selected。

 How much information have they given you about the selection。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_8.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_9.png)

The probability of a man being selected in this problem is 3 out of 8。

 So the amount of information that you have been given is log base 2 of1 over 3 out of 8。

 That's equivalent to log base2 of 8 over 3。

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_11.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_12.png)

Another way to think about this is that if you start off with n choices and the additional information that you are given reduces your choices to M。

 then the amount of information that you have been given is logbase 2 of n over M。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_14.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_15.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_16.png)

In this example， n equals 8， because that is the original number of choices。

 The reduced number of choices。 once you are told that a man has been selected， is 3。 so M equals 3。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_18.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_19.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_20.png)

This means that the amount of information you received is log base2 of8 over 3。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_22.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_23.png)

For the second question， you are asked to guess a random4 B2s complement number。

 I then tell you that the number is greater than 0。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_25.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_26.png)

How much information have you been given？For this problem。

 the original number of choices is the number of permutations of four binary bits， which is 16。

 So n equals 16。

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_28.png)

![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_29.png)

Once you are told that the number is greater than 0， this reduces your choice to 7。 So M equals 7。



![](img/44153926169577ba52cd46be1691f2e8_31.png)

That means that the amount of information that you have been given is log base 2 of 16 over 7。
