Progressive discipline is a form of organizational due process due process is a legal concept。

 but the general idea can be applied in an organizational setting。

 an organization that employs due process is committing itself to make decisions and take actions relating to employment according to pre-estlished procedures。

According to due process， employees will be notified of impending actions and will be allowed to defend themselves if necessary before any employment decisions are made。

There are two common reasons for dismissing an employee The first is misconduct。

 typically when an employee violates workplace rules or a code of conduct。

 misconduct can include gross misconduct， behavior that is harmful to others or crimes like theft。

A second reason for dismissing an employee is unsatisfactory performance when the employee is proven to be unqualified or consistently fails to perform at the standard set by the organization。

 there are grounds for dismissal。It is essential that the organization keep documentation of weak performance and efforts to improve it。

A progressive discipline system has several clear advantages。 First。

 when supervisors follow the progressive sequence， they communicate both the nature and seriousness of the problem to the employee。

 Second， supervisors always know the right disciplinary step to take next。

 There is no second guessing， which reduces the stress and awkwardness that often accompaniess workplace discipline。

 That said， supervisors might choose not to follow all steps in the progressive discipline system。

 Some very serious offenses。 such as theft or alcohol or drug use at work。

 can be cause for immediate termination。 But in general。

 if the organization has a progressive discipline policy， supervisors should follow it。

 Several court decisions have ruled that failure to follow written policies for progressive discipline could render an employee's dismissal invalid。
