There are five steps in the learning and development process。Training needs analysis， objectives。

 selection， implementation， and evaluation。

![](img/cf5c7390bb4463744e00a2c17eb26d2a_4.png)

Let's define them all in a bit more detail First， a training needs analysis or TNA helps HR teams identify employee training goals by examining areas in the organization that need improvement。

It also considers training requested directly by employees， for example。

 your HR team conducts a survey， the survey identifies workers who would like more training and what the topics should include。

These assessments inform the needs analysis and help identify which type of training is appropriate。

The next step objectives defines training or development goals。

 the goals should include impact objectives， which specify how the training will impact the performance of the organization。

For example， your organization has implemented a new software program and employees are asking for additional training you offer training sessions and a Q&A about the software The impact objective of this training is that after completing it。

 employees will be more confident and efficient using the software for their tasks Select is the next step Select determines who should receive the training or development and what delivery methods should be used。

In our software example， let's say you invite a representative from the software company or third party trainer to present a seminar about the product。

 only employees who use a software will attend the training。

The fourth stage is implementation implementation is how a training or development should be executed。

There are many different ways to implement a training plan。 For example。

 one team may need a classroom style training session and others might require a simulation of the skills or knowledge to succeed。

 It depends on the need， the topic and the team。The final step in a learning and development plan is evaluation evaluation determines the effectiveness of the training or development。

For example， you can evaluate if the software training sessions were effective by comparing task completion times before and after the training。
