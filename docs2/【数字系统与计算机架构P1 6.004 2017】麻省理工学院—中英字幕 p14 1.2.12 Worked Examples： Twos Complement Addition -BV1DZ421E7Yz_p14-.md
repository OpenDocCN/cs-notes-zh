。The nice feature of two's complement representation is that it allows you to do both addition and subtraction as addition problems by simply turning the subtraction problem A minus B into an addition problem of a plus negative B。

 Since we now know how to easily negate values in two's complement。 This is pretty straightforward。😊。

For example， let's perform 1518 using6 bit2s complement representation。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_7.png)

1518 is equal to 15 plus a negative 18。And 15 is equal to 0，0，1，1，1，1。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_9.png)

18 is equal to0，1，0，0，1，0。To convert 18 to 18， we flip all the bits and add one。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_11.png)

This results in 1，0，1，1，1，0， which is equal to -18。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_13.png)

We can now take our twos complement representation of our two numbers and add them together。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_15.png)

Whenever we add one plus one in binary， that produces a0 plus a carry of one。 So our final sum is 1。

1，1，1，0，1。

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_17.png)

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_18.png)

To see what this number is equal to， we again flip the bits and add one。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_20.png)

Flipping the bits， we get 0，0，0，0，1，0。 and adding  one to that， we get 0，0，0，0，1，1， which equals 3。

 which means that 1，1，1，1，0，1， is equal to negative 3。

 which is the result we expect it to get when performing 15-18。

If you try adding two numbers whose result is beyond the range of numbers that can be represented using your endbit representation。

 then that means that overflow occurred。

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_43.png)

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_44.png)

Overflow can be detected by looking at the sign of the two numbers being added and the sign of the resulting number。

 If you try adding two positive numbers where the most significant bit is 0 and your result ends up being negative。

 Your most significant bit is a1。 Then overflow occurred。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_46.png)

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_47.png)

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_48.png)

Similarly， if you try adding two negative numbers whose most significant bit is one and you end up with a result that is positive。

 where the most significant bit is 0， then once again， overflow occurred。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_50.png)

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_51.png)

Overflow cannot occur when you add a positive and negative number that are within range。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_53.png)

Let's take a look at an example of this。 Suppose we try adding 31 plus 12 using 6 bit2s complement。



![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_55.png)

This results in 10，10，11。Which has its most significant bit equal to one。

 even though we were adding two positive numbers， This means that overflow occurred。

![](img/c7eb6eeaad547efc5f7a66f111aab486_58.png)

 which is larger than 2 to the  fifth-1 or 31， which is the largest positive number that can be represented using6 B2s complement。
