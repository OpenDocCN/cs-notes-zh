So far， you have learned about the behavioral， comparative。

 and rating methods for performance appraisals。Now we are going to review the narrative method。

 which is the final performance appraisal method。

![](img/7460f70ccf1b3b8de8c943fc6f54b5a8_2.png)

The narrative method requires managers or HR representatives to write a review of each employee's performance。

The three types of narrative reviews are critical incident review， essay review， and field review。

Critical incident review involves a manager recording specific incidents in which an employee performed positively or negatively。

 The incidents then form the basis of a written narrative that is discussed with the employee during their performance appraisal。

 for example， Blake， a line cook at slicelice you was recently observed not washing their hands before a shift。

 wearing their hair down and not wearing gloves when preparing food。

 Blake's manager Riley wrote a critical incident review for Blake and informed them during a meeting that they will need to repeat the organization's food safety training。

 If the incident happens again， Blake's employment will be terminated。😊，In an essay review。

 a manager writes a short essay describing an employee's performance throughout the review period with this format。

 managers are more easily able to identify areas that are in need of improvement。As an example。

 connective managers use this method for yearly performance appraisals。

 They use data from informal reviews to create essay reviews for each employee。

 The essay reviews are then shared with the employee during their appraisal。 Finally。

 a field review is conducted by someone who is not an employee's manager or supervisor。

 This is typically conducted by a human resource professional from the organization or department or someone from outside of the organization or department。

😊，This method is useful for managerial level promotions。
