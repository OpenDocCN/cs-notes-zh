It's important to note that each job analysis approach has pros and cons。

Let's begin with interviews。 When you think of an interview between two people。

 you picture quite a direct interaction， right， One person asks questions and the other answers them。

 Interviews can either be structured or unstructured。



![](img/a9ce5214c1fe4fe43bc9eef801ddbaa0_4.png)

A structured interview uses a detailed checklist to ensure the interviewer covers all the necessary topics during the interview In comparison。

 unstructured interviews use open- ended questions to allow the employee to provide in- depth responses based on their experiences。

 For example， you might ask， what are the most time sensitive responsibilities and how do you prioritize them This type of question allows the employee to respond freely and for the interviewer to ask follow up questions。

😊，Interviews are often quite simple to conduct The employees responses can reveal job duties that may be unassigned or missed。

 This provides an advantage to your HR team as you can use this information to accurately develop or update a job description At the same time。

 there are disadvantages to interviewing any misrepresentation during an interview could negatively affect the quality of the information you gather and the overall job analysis process。

 For example， an employee might exaggerate the expertise or knowledge required for the job to make it appear more challenging or desirable。

😊。

Questionaires like interviews， are common in job analysis and can be either unstructured or structured。

 Ques can be distorted by misrepresentations。 Let's look at a type of structured questionnaire。

The position analysis questionnaire or PAQ is a tool that includes 194 job related questions。

 These questions are used to gain an understanding of a specific job。

 The PAQ is completed by either an employee or supervisor in their responses they describe and record information about the specific tasks。

 activities and competencies required to perform the role。



![](img/a9ce5214c1fe4fe43bc9eef801ddbaa0_8.png)

You can also use standardized words provided by the US Department of Labor to create clear and accurate questions for a structured questionnaire。

These consistent terms ensure the questionnaire captures the necessary information for each role。

The last method we will discuss is observation In this method。

 you directly observe an employee performing their job。

 You collect data about the tasks the worker completes and the skills they use。

 This method is the most accurate because it involves firsthand observation and provides a clear understanding of the job requirements。

 There is a downside， however， to this method。 observation， like interviews， costs money and time。

 It's important to keep the pros and cons of each method in line。
