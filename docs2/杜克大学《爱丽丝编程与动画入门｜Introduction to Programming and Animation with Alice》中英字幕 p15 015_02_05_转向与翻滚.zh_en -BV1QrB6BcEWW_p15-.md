In this session， we' further explore the turn in roll procedures。

 Many students quickly get quite comfortable with Alice3's directions of movement。

 We found that it often takes a bit more practice to get used to rotation。 Again。

 we've prebuilt an Alice project。 We have three objects， a treasure chest。

 a microwave oven and a grandfather clock。 Let's see how the lid turns on a treasure chest to open。

 The lid needs to be turned backward。 Then to close， it can be turned forward。😊。

The door in a microwave oven works differently than the lid on a treasure chest。

 It hinges on the microwave's right side or the camera's left side to open the door。

 the door has to be turned right and then turn left to close the door。

The hands on a grandfather' clock need to be rolled rather than turned to roll the minute hand to have time advanced。

 the minute hand is rolled to its left。For time to go back。

 the minute at hand gets rolled to its right。It will likely be worthwhile for you to run this Alice project a few times to get more accustomed to how turning and rolling work in Alice。
