In this demonstration， we'll explore the differences between performing animation instructions in order versus performing animation instructions together。

Again， we have prebuilt an Alice project。 There are two monkeys each facing a palm tree。

 The monkey on the camera's left has been given the unimaginative name， left monkey。

 and the monkey on the right has been given the equally unimaginative name， right monkey。

Let's look at the effect of asking the monkeys to turn around half revolution to the left in order。

First， the left monkey turns around， and afterwards， the right monkey turns around。Now。

 let's look at the effect of having the monkeys turn around together。 Notice the difference。

 rather than waiting for the first monkey to turn around before the second monkey does。 Instead。

 they turn around together at the same time。For the next part。

 we get a chance to see how do together can actually result in different positioning of objects after the animation has run。

First， we in perform instructions with a do order with a monkey on the right。

 It moves forward two units。 After completing the move， it then turns right a quarter revolution。

 The net result of this sequence of animation instructions。

 Run in order is that the monkey is immediately in front of the palm tree。

 It is turned to the right as a result of the second instruction。 The turn instructions。

We now perform the exact same instructions for the monkey on the left with a do together。

For the monkey on the left， it moves forward。 At the same time。

 it is turning a quarter revolution to its right。 Notice the difference。 In this case。

 the left monkey traces what appears to be a quarter of a circle to its right。

 It ends up facing to the right in the same direction as the right monkey。

But it's located to the right of its palm tree and also not as far forward。

 It's a bit harder to see this， not as close to the tree as the monkey on the right。

 It is rather easy to see that it is further forward。

It is worthwhile to run this Alice project a few times to see the differences between do in order and do together。
