Job design can improve workers experiences as well as organizational efficiency。

 The five different approaches to job design are mechanistic motivational biological perceptual motor and relational。

 Let's start with a mechanistic approach。 The mechanistic approach to job design is based on classic industrial engineering concepts and share some common things with operations research。

 It focuses on evaluating the simple， repetitive tasks of a specific job。

 It is more applicable to jobs that involve assembly like those found in a manufacturing firm。😊。



![](img/eb3112371f4cb62a2cd5747bcde1443f_3.png)

Certain office jobs and even telemarketing can also be designed according to the mechanistic approach because they demand a high level of consistency。

The mechanistic approach can help you design jobs that require simple training。

 maximum efficiency and productivity and reduce error rates。

 This approach does not necessarily encourage job satisfaction or motivation。 However。

 when workers perform the same tasks over and over。

 They may potentially become bored and unmotivated。

 These feelings can lead to high absenteeism and turnover。😊，Next is a motivational approach。

 The motivational approach emphasizes the idea that workers are more satisfied with their jobs if they feel a sense of control and are able to use their skills and understand the results of their work。

 you can use the motivational approach to redesign jobs that result in the high level of worker satisfaction。

 This approach is best suited。 however， for jobs that entail extensive training and for workers who are able to be creative and innovative in their position。

😊，The significant variations in jobs designed with a motivational approach may create negative results your organization may incur higher training and recruiting costs for these specialized jobs。

😊，It may also encounter lower productivity and propensity for errors。

These potential impacts should be carefully considered when choosing a job design approach。Next。

 let's cover the biological approach。 The biological approach is rooted in ergonomics。

 The study of how workers use machines and equipment for maximum performance。

 This approach is most relevant to jobs that are physically demanding。

This approach considers workers comfort。 It evaluates the equipment they use。

 the noise and temperature of the work environment， or the time spent on the job。😊。

If your goal is to address health and wellness in job design， the biological approach is helpful。

Adjustments to the equipment and work environment can be costly， however。

Next is the perceptual motor approach。 This approach emphasizes the mental demands of a particular job。

 It identifies the attention span and memory required to successfully perform the job。

The perceptual motor approach aims to balance cognitive requirements of the job with the skills of the worker。

 This balance can reduce stress and boredom， resulting in fewer errors or accidents。😊。

A disadvantage to this approach is that it does not consider some of the concerns of the motivational approach。

 which may cause a worker to find their job less meaningful。Finally。

 let's discuss the relational approach The relational approach evaluates a specific job stage or site This evaluation focuses on the position of job tasks within a process and the relationships of workers to each other and then end user。

 although this approach was pioneered by newton Wellesly hospital in its care of hospital patients。

 it can be applied to projects in education and consulting work the relational approach can help you emphasize timely。

 accurate communication and collaboration across teams。

 it can also motivate workers when they realize how their work impacts others。😊。

This can be a time consuming approach， however， since it requires frequent communication and inquiries。
