Welcome to the final project， where you'll practice using many of the tools and techniques you've learned for image processing。



![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_3.png)

In this scenario， a company has been struggling with heavy traffic outside of its office building。

They've hired you to measure traffic volume throughout the day。To get you started。

 they provided you with this short video clip taken from a camera they have set up on the roof。

Your goal is to count the number of cars in each frame， as they drive by。

This project is divided into three steps。

![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_5.png)

First， you'll pre processces the video by removing the noise and converting it to grayscale。



![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_7.png)

Next， you'll isolate the cars in each frame by creating a binary mask that segments them from the background。



![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_9.png)

![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_10.png)

You'll likely encounter some interesting challenges during this step。For instance， in this frame。

 the telephone pole splits a car into two regions。

![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_12.png)

So you'll need to find a way to join them together。



![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_14.png)

You'll then use the regions in each frame to calculate various properties and analyze the results for the entire video。



![](img/60df6b2679d8879d6aad962b850d1ec1_16.png)

Finally， you'll modify the original video by overlaying a bounding box to track the cars as they drive by。

Assessments will be used to guide your progress after each step。

 They act as checkpoints and can be taken any number of times。

 So make sure you pass them 100 per cent before moving on。Like most image processing problems。

 there's no single correct solution， so everyone's results will likely be a bit different。
