This is a fun lecture that shows you how to take your favorite Alice object and turn it into a scrumptious cookie。

I've been making Alice cookies for my class at Duke for years。

 so I will pass on some tips that I have found。

![](img/a340d3bc2ea369417f574c40b93ff9c4_2.png)

Here is the penguin in Alice and some penguin cookies I made back in April。

 I made over 60 cookies for my class at Duke。 Let's see how to make such cookies。 First。

 pick a character in Alice to make a cookie of。 I have chosen the pig。

 There are lots of ways to make a cookie that is the same shape as this pig。😊。

We will mention three different ways。 One way is to create a cookie cutter with a 3D printer。

A second way is to cut the cookie out with a knife。

And the third way is to make a cookie cutter by bending a thick metal strip。

We will focus mostly on making a 3D cookie cutter。There are lots of websites that you can use to create a 3D cookie cutter。

 if you don't have access to a 3D printer， then you can ship your design to places that will print your 3D cookie cutter for you。

I use the website， cookiecastster or www。cookiecastster。com to design a pig cookie cutter。



![](img/a340d3bc2ea369417f574c40b93ff9c4_4.png)

To make the pig cookie cutter， I first put a pig into an Alice project。

Then I went into the scene setup。I painted the pig black to get a good contrast。

I made the ground invisible， setting its opacity to zero。

I selected the scene and sent the atmosphere color to white。

That gives a great contrast of a black pig on a white background。

I then took a picture of the black pig and uploaded the image to cookiecaststor。com。



![](img/a340d3bc2ea369417f574c40b93ff9c4_6.png)

It will trace images that have good contrast。

![](img/a340d3bc2ea369417f574c40b93ff9c4_8.png)

I clicked on the pig and cookiecastster automatically draws the cookie cutter shape around the pig。

Then I downloaded a STL file of the cookie cutter。You then need to find a 3D printer and convert your dot STL file into a file that your 3D printer understands in the process。

 you can see what the 3D cookie cutter will look like before you print it。

Here's a picture of printing the 3D cookie cutter。It took over three hours to print。

 I ended up making two cookie cutters of the pig in different sizes。 And here they are。



![](img/a340d3bc2ea369417f574c40b93ff9c4_10.png)

Now it is time to bake the cookies。 You need to make the dough。

 My favorite recipe is a simple cookie called sand tart that has flour， sugar， butter， eggs， vanilla。

 and a few other things。😊。

![](img/a340d3bc2ea369417f574c40b93ff9c4_12.png)

I got the recipe from a good housekeeping magazine in December，1996。

I always make two batches of the dough at the same time， so I use two bowls。

Each batch I split into three balls of dough。 Here are two of them。

You can freeze the dough for days until you're ready to use it。

If I didn't have access to 3D printing， which I didn't have access for many years。

 then I would cut out the picture of the pig and use that to cut out the pig shapes。

Another alternative is to make your own cookie cutter using a kit of metal strips like this one。

Then you would bend the metal strip into the shape you want。Now that the dough is ready。

 I prepare to make the cookies。 I clear an area on my table。

 pull out my baking pans and my rolling pen and put some extra flour on the table。

 I also prepare a place to put the cookies when they're done baking。

 putting down sheets of wax paper。😊，If you wet the table first。

 the wax paper will stick to the table。After the dough is thawed a bit。

 or use the microwave to thoughtaw quicker， but be careful not to thoughtaw too much。

I roll out the dough。 Now I could cut the cookie here and then try to transfer it to the cookie sheet。

 but the cookie shape can mess up in the transfer。 It's easier to transfer the dough to the cookie sheet and cut the cookie on the cookie sheet。

Now that the dough is on the cookie sheet， I will cut out one cookie by hand， which is， again。

 how I made Alice's cookies for years。Just put the picture on the dough and use a sharp knife to cut around the shape。

If you can make a 3D cookie cutter， it is so much faster。

 Just press the cookie cutter into the dough。 I strongly recommend making your own cookie cutter if you plan to make a lot of cookies。

😊，Moving a big round of flatten dough can be hard。 It tears easily。

 so instead you can cut pieces the correct size and then lay those pieces on the cookie sheet。

Pres your cookie cutter into the dough and you have a cookie。

Here we have a tray of cookies ready to go into the oven to bake。

 takes about 16 minutes to bake one tray of cookies。

 and here we have finished cookies cooling on the wax paper on the table。They call pretty quickly。

 and they smell so good。 Watch out。 They tend to disappear if you're not looking。

 and there are others in the house。Next， we will decorate the cookies。 Get icing。

 I use white icing mostly。 It has to be white。 Van icing has a tent of yellow in it。

 If you add another color like red， that yellow will turn your red to orange。

 So be sure to get white icing。Craft stores usually sell white icing if you cannot find it at a grocery store。

If I need the color black， I find that it is easiest to use chocolateicing and add black food coloring to it。

If you add black fig coloring to white， you need to put a huge amount to turn the icing black。

For this pig， we need the colors pink for the body。Black for the hooves。

 gray for the nostrils and white and blue for the eyes。I like to use food gel colors。

 They are thicker and don't water down the icing。First。
