A large component of a manager's role includes supervisory tasks。

Managers must closely monitor employees performance。

 offer constructive feedback and acknowledge employees efforts These tasks are known as performance management。

😊。

![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_2.png)

Performance management is a process of setting goals， measuring progress。

 and rewarding or correcting employees performance。



![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_4.png)

This process typically follows three steps。First， clearly defining the actions and behaviors required to achieve organizational goals Next。

 regularly assess whether employees meet these expectations， and last。

 take necessary measures to adjust or maintain current performance levels based on the assessment。😊。



![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_6.png)

Many organizations streamline this process by using a performance management system。

A performance management system helps people perform to the best of their abilities。

 it begins by defining a job and ends when an employee leaves the organization。😊。



![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_8.png)

Performance management systems help create a productive work environment where all employees are treated equally。

Most performance management systems consist of common elements， let's discuss a few of them。

Goals are a fundamental element of performance management systems。

 it is not enough for managers to simply understand their organization's goals however。

 a performance management system requires a more specific and focused approach for example。

 if an organization aims to increase profits by 10% next year。

 managers must ask questions like how much do sales need to improve by how much can cost increase and what actions do we need to take to reach this goal。

😊。

![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_10.png)

![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_11.png)

The next element is an evaluation structure。Typically。

 employees participate in a structured annual written evaluation that compares their work to the organization's expectations to create a more complete appraisal of an employee's performance。

 other evaluation structures including rating scales， manager input， peer reviews。

 and self-asses are used。😊，We'll discuss some of these methods in further detail this week。

Corrective action or course corrections play a vital role in performance management systems。

This element helps employees improve their performance and allows organizations to identify and plan for the development of top performers。

Managers are also able to identify necessary changes within the organization and adjust their expectations of employees accordingly。

😊，Benchmarking is a valuable method for analyzing performance and establishing standards in an organization。

External benchmarking compares employee performance to employees at other organizations。

 Internal benchmarking examines and compares the organization's past performance to the present。😊。

Benchmarking techniques enable performance management systems to set baseline expectations and goals for continuous improvement。

![](img/f13d0d6778fe51a0c83e070588f07a57_14.png)

Each of these elements of performance management systems helps ensure management tasks are performed effectively throughout an organization。

You now have an understanding of the different components that make up a performance management system and their importance in driving an organization's success。
